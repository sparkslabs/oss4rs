## What is open source software?

According to opensource.com, Open source software is: "software with source code that anyone can inspect, modify, and enhance".

Many open source projects are developed collaboratively online between developers from across the world.

Open source software is usually distributed free of charge, but a charge might be applied (or a donation sought) to cover the 
cost of the server providing the download or to support development costs.

Many companies release code that is open source as it can be beneificial to take contributions and improvements from users. 
It can also help to sell associated services or hardware.

### Why should you use open source to run a workshop, conference or research project?

#### Cost

Open source software is usually available without any fee. We "just" need to have somewhere 
to run it.

#### Customisation

Open source software is inherently customistable to your needs. This means you will be able to disable features you don't want/need or integrate systems together.
This will come at a cost of time and having the required expertise to do so. 

#### Data Sovereignty

Increasingly we store our data including documents, emails and research data on cloud computing systems run by large technology companies that are usually based in the USA.
In recent years there has been growing concerns over what jurisidiction data is hosted in. Typically the authorities of that country will be able to request
access to that data, even if the country of the data's subject or owner would not agree to this. There are also concerns that services could be pulled
or data removed at the request of a government, crippling organisations who are dependent on that data or those services.

With open source solutions we are able to host services on our own infrastructure or with a domestic cloud provider. 

#### Removing Dependence on Big Tech Companies

Our research is increasingly dependent on a small number of big tech companies. Many people have concerns about the ethics of some of these companies.
Of particular concern to many has been push towards generative AI, the use of customer data to train those AI systems and the environmental impacts of this.

##### Keeping skills and funding local

Using big tech companies also means that most of the people who are employed to maintain the systems and software that you are using aren't local to you
and are probably located in a different country. If you are spending public money on these services then that money is unlikely to be paying anybody in
your local community or perhaps even in your country. By using an open source solution that money can be directed to local providers or even people in your
institution keeping the economic benefits of that spending closer to you.

### Why open source might not be suitable for you

Open source software isn't always going to be suitable.

* Your organisation might already have contracts and agreements with other suppliers and they will prefer or even require you to use them.
* The open source software will require installing and hosting somewhere. This often needs more expertise than an off the shelf system from a big tech company.
* Open source software isn't always as complete, functional or well polished as the closed source alternatives.
